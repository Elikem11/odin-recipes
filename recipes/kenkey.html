Kenkay

Description

Kenkey is a typical Ghanaian dish made from fermented white corn which is widely consumed throughout the country. It is believed to have originated by Ga people from southern Ghana or Accra.

The Ga tribe calls it 'Komi' and the Fantes call it 'Dokono'. Kenkey is often accompanied by fried fish throughout West Africa where fishing is widely practiced and where fish are generally eaten fresh and fried or dried or smoked for better preservation

Ingredients

  • Fermented corn dough
  • Corn husks
  • Water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Get your corn husks, soak and wash them very well
  2. Soak them in hot water for 5 minutes and allow to dry.
  3. Put half of the corn dough in a bowl and mix with water
  4. Boil corn dough until half way cooked
  5. Spread the dough on a large clean saucepan and add the remaining raw corn dough using a wooden ladle.
  6. Mix together thoroughly.
  7. Mould into preferred pieces/size.
  8. Wrap each with the corn husks
  9. Place some corn husks at the bottom of the pot and arrange the wrapped dough nicely in it.
  10. Add enough water, cover and boil for about an hour
  11. Check to see if cooked to your desired specification(hard/soft)
  12. Serve with fried fish, or meat, hot pepper sauce, chilli sauce, stew or shito. Enjoy
